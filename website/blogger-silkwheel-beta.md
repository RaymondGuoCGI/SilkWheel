# I Built SilkWheel, a Small Windows App That Makes Mouse Wheel Scrolling Feel Smoother

I recently built a small Windows utility called **SilkWheel**.

The idea is simple: when you use a regular mouse wheel on Windows, scrolling often feels stepped, jumpy, or a little harsh. You turn the wheel once, and the page moves in obvious chunks.

SilkWheel tries to make that experience feel calmer, smoother, and more controlled.

Instead of letting every wheel notch jump the page immediately, SilkWheel catches the wheel input and turns it into a short, smooth glide. The goal is not to make scrolling flashy. The goal is to make everyday reading, browsing, and document work feel more comfortable.

## Why I Built It

I use a mouse a lot, especially when browsing long web pages, reading documentation, working with files, and switching between different desktop apps.

One thing that always bothered me is that Windows mouse wheel scrolling can feel very mechanical. Touchpads can feel smooth, but a normal mouse wheel often feels like it moves in steps.

So I started building SilkWheel as a lightweight tray app for Windows. It runs in the background, lets you tune the scrolling feel, and can be paused or adjusted whenever needed.

## What SilkWheel Does

SilkWheel is focused on a few practical things:

- smoother vertical mouse wheel scrolling
- quick profile switching for different scrolling feels
- per-app exclusions when a specific app should keep native scrolling
- tray control, startup option, and simple settings
- light, gray, dark, and custom themes

It is designed to stay out of the way. You install it, choose a feel that works for you, and let it run from the system tray.

## Current Beta

SilkWheel is currently in public beta.

You can use the beta for free for 21 days. After that, I only ask for one short piece of real feedback to keep using the current beta version.

That feedback matters more than early payment right now. I want to learn:

- which apps people use SilkWheel with
- which mice feel better or worse
- whether the scrolling feels too fast, too slow, or still jumpy
- what default settings feel best for real users

Once the product is more mature, I may consider a more advanced version. But for now, the goal is simple: get real users, listen carefully, and improve the scrolling feel.
